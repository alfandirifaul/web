Kellogg’s, a leading American producer of ready-to-eat cereals and other food products, is headquartered in Battle Creek, Michigan. Founded in 1900 as the Sanitas Food Company by brothers W.K. Kellogg and Dr. John H. Kellogg, they developed Corn Flakes, one of the earliest and most popular breakfast cereals in the U.S. W.K. Kellogg later bought out his brother and established the Battle Creek Toasted Corn Flake Company in 1906. The company thrived through innovative advertising and quality improvements, adopting the name Kellogg’s in 1922 as it expanded beyond cornflakes.
